What to listen for in the recording

When you upload your test recording for us to review together, listen for:

  • Where you felt rushed — usually means we packed too much in that section
  • Where they were quiet — usually means they didn't follow you
  • Where they laughed or got excited — usually means we found something to lean into more
  • Questions you couldn't answer cleanly — those are content gaps for module v2
  • Pacing of the practice section — was 15 minutes enough? Too much?

Don't try to analyze it yourself first. Just upload the audio. Fresh ears catch what tired ears miss.

What to NOT worry about for cohort 1

You'll be tempted to fix everything. Resist. Things that are fine to be imperfect on Tuesday:

  • Hosting on a custom domain (we have github.io URLs for now; quintaand.co will come later)
  • The terms page not existing yet (no money, no terms needed)
  • Email automation not built (manual emails to 3-5 people is faster than building automation)
  • The signup form not being public (friends are invited directly)
  • Cohort Brief feature not built (you know your friends already)

Save the energy for showing up well in the room.

Two days from now, women you care about are going to spend an hour with you because they trust you. They are not grading you. They are rooting for you. Whatever happens — the perfect parts and the awkward parts — that's the data we use to make next time better. There's no version of this where you fail; there's only "I learned something" or "I learned something AND it went really well."

Go teach.
