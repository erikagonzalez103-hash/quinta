# Faculty 1099s — Step-by-Step Guide

*For Quinta & Co. faculty paid as independent contractors. Educational summary, not tax advice — confirm specifics with your accountant.*

## The one-sentence version

Collect a W-9 from every instructor **before their first payment**, track what you pay each person during the year, and by **January 31** send anyone you paid $600+ a **1099-NEC** and file a copy with the IRS.

## Step 1 — Collect a W-9 at onboarding (now, not in January)

- Every instructor fills out [IRS Form W-9](https://www.irs.gov/forms-pubs/about-form-w-9) — it gives you their legal name, business name, entity type, address, and tax ID.
- Make it part of the faculty intake packet, alongside the instructor agreement and media release.
- Store it privately (never in this public repo). Don't accept SSNs over plain email if avoidable.
- **Why entity type matters:** payments to corporations (C-corp or S-corp per their W-9) are generally *exempt* from 1099-NEC — **except attorneys, who get one regardless**. Watch this for Nik Sallie if the trademark/estate classes are taught through a law practice.

## Step 2 — Track payments per person, all year

- Keep a simple running total per instructor per calendar year (a column in the faculty tracker or ledger).
- Threshold: **$600 or more in the calendar year** = 1099-NEC required.

## Step 3 — Pay attention to *how* you pay (this changes who files)

- Payments by **check, Zelle, ACH/bank transfer, or cash** → **you** report them on a 1099-NEC.
- Payments by **credit card, PayPal, Venmo-business, Square, Stripe** → the **processor** reports them on a 1099-K, and you should **not** also issue a 1099-NEC for those amounts (that double-reports their income).
- If you mix methods for one person, only the check/Zelle/ACH portion goes on their 1099-NEC.

## Step 4 — December: confirm details

- Verify each instructor's mailing address and W-9 info is current.
- Total up what you paid each person and flag everyone at $600+.

## Step 5 — File by January 31

Both deadlines are the same day, **January 31**:
1. **Copy B to the instructor** (mail or, with their consent, electronically).
2. **Copy A to the IRS** — e-file it. Options:
   - **IRS IRIS portal** (free, at irs.gov/iris) — but it requires a Transmitter Control Code (TCC) that can take **up to 45 days to get, so apply in the fall**.
   - **A filing service** (Track1099, Tax1099, QuickBooks) — roughly $3–5 per form, no TCC needed, they mail recipient copies for you. With ~8 faculty this is ~$30/year and the easy button.
- Note: businesses filing **10 or more** information returns total must e-file — e-file regardless.

## Step 6 — State filing

- **Texas has no state income tax → no Texas state 1099 filing.**
- If an instructor **lives in another state**, that state may want a copy — filing services usually handle this automatically ("combined federal/state filing"). Just answer their state questions honestly.

## Step 7 — Keep records

Keep W-9s, payment records, and filed 1099 copies for **at least 4 years**.

## Calendar summary

| When | What |
|---|---|
| At onboarding | W-9 signed + stored, before first payment |
| All year | Track per-person totals; note payment method |
| October | Apply for IRIS TCC (or pick a filing service) |
| December | Confirm addresses, total everyone up |
| **January 31** | 1099-NEC copies to instructors AND filed with IRS |
