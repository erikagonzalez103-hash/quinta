Getting set up in Gusto

Gusto is the payroll service Quinta & Co. uses to pay faculty. Their onboarding isn't the friendliest, so here's the whole thing in plain language — it takes about ten minutes, once, and then you never think about it again.

Have these three things ready before you start

  • Your tax ID — your SSN if you're being paid personally, or your business EIN if you're being paid through your LLC or corporation. (Not sure which? See the FAQ below.)
  • Your legal name and address — exactly as they appear on your tax return.
  • A voided check or your banking login — you'll need your routing and account numbers for direct deposit.

Tip: print this page or keep it open on your phone while you go.

  1. Watch for an email from Gusto

    Once Erika adds you, Gusto sends an invitation from a gusto.com address asking you to complete your profile. If it hasn't arrived within a day, check spam — and if it's truly missing, email hello@quintaand.co and we'll resend it.

  2. Click the link and create your account

    Set a password. One quirk of Gusto: if it asks you to sign in and you've never set a password, don't panic — click "Forgot password" and it will let you create one. (Yes, really. This trips up everyone.)

  3. Enter your tax details — this is your W-9

    Gusto asks for your legal name, address, and SSN or EIN. This screen is the W-9 — filling it out here means there's no paper form to sign or email around, and your information stays encrypted with a payroll company instead of sitting in an inbox.

  4. Add your bank account

    Routing number and account number, for direct deposit. Gusto may verify the account instantly through your bank login, or with small test deposits that take a day or two.

  5. Review and finish

    Confirm everything and submit. Erika gets a notification that you're done — there's nothing to send her. That's it: you're set up to be paid.

Questions, answered

Gusto FAQ for Quinta & Co. faculty

1

Is it safe to type my Social Security number in here?

Yes — this is the reason we use Gusto instead of emailing W-9 forms around. Gusto is one of the largest payroll companies in the U.S. and stores tax IDs encrypted, the same way your bank stores yours. An SSN in Gusto is far safer than an SSN in an email attachment.

2

Should I set up as an individual or a business?

If Quinta & Co. is paying you, choose individual and use your SSN. If we're paying your company (you invoice through an LLC or corporation with its own EIN), choose business. If you're not sure, a quick question to your accountant settles it — and your answer here should match how you report the income.

3

Does this cost me anything?

No. Quinta & Co. pays for Gusto. You'll never be charged for anything.

4

When and how do I get paid?

By direct deposit to the account you added, on the schedule in your instructor agreement. You'll get an email from Gusto each time a payment is on its way, and you can see your full payment history any time by logging in.

5

What about my 1099?

If we pay you $600 or more in a calendar year, Gusto prepares your 1099-NEC automatically, files it with the IRS, and emails you a copy in January. Nothing to request, nothing to chase.

6

I already have a Gusto account from another job.

Use the same login — Gusto links your work with multiple companies under one account, as long as the invitation went to the same email address. If it went to a different email, tell Erika which one you'd rather use.

7

My invitation link expired, or I'm locked out.

Go to gusto.com, click Sign in, then "Forgot password" with the email your invite came to. That recovers almost every stuck state. If Gusto still won't cooperate, email hello@quintaand.co and Erika will resend the invite.

8

Who can see my information?

Gusto, and Erika as the account admin (she can see payment amounts and onboarding status — not your bank login). Nothing from Gusto ever appears on the Quinta & Co. website.
